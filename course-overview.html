Digital Innovations

An AI Literacy Programme for the Age of Artificial Intelligence
COURSE OVERVIEW
Haileybury · 2026

Digital Innovations is Haileybury's artificial-intelligence literacy programme. It teaches pupils not just how to use AI tools, but how to understand, question and apply them responsibly. The whole course runs through an interactive web app — nothing to install, works offline, and free to operate — across three pathways tailored to different ages and goals, from a self-directed introduction to a full Academic Extension Programme with an independent build project.

Three pathways, one programme

Pathway Best for Lessons What it delivers
Digital Innovations AEP Sixth-Form & academic extension 59 · 6 units The complete programme — theory, ethics, hands-on skills and an independent AI build, assessed by presentation & viva
Removes Younger pupils (Removes year) 34 · 6 units AI literacy and smart study skills, in short, friendly lessons
AI Fluency Track Any pupil or staff member, self-paced 20 · 5 stages A no-login, self-directed route to confident, critical everyday AI use

What pupils gain

●  Understand how AI really works — from how chatbots predict text to what reasoning models and AI agents can (and cannot) do

●  Write effective prompts and use AI as a genuine thinking partner, not a shortcut

●  Critically evaluate and fact-check AI output, and spot deepfakes and misinformation

●  Weigh the ethics — bias, privacy, environmental and human costs, copyright and AI companions

●  Engage with policy and regulation, and form their own evidence-based positions

●  (AEP) Design, build, ethically audit and pitch their own AI-powered project

A flavour of what we cover

Lessons tackle real, current questions — not abstractions. A sample of what pupils actually grapple with:

How AI really works

The Parrot vs. the Librarian — does AI understand, or just predict the next word?

Tokens & the Strawberry Problem — why AI miscounts the letters in a word, and what that reveals

Reasoning models — o3, GPT-5-thinking, Claude & DeepSeek R1: thinking before they answer

Agents, function calling & MCP — when AI stops chatting and starts doing

The big ethical questions

The Replika “apocalypse” — when a company changed millions of users' AI companions overnight

Regulating AI companions — after the death of a 14-year-old user, how would you legislate?

The hidden costs — nuclear-powered data centres, and workers paid $1.32/hr to label trauma

NYT v OpenAI — who owns what an AI learned from?
Using AI with skill

The PTFC framework — Persona, Task, Format, Context: prompting that actually works

Trust, but verify — the four kinds of AI hallucination and how to catch them

Prompt injection — the No. 1 security flaw in AI applications

RAG — grounding AI in real documents instead of guesswork

AI, society & your future

Deepfakes, surveillance & facial recognition — synthetic media vs. civil liberties

AI & democracy — micro-targeting, influence operations and elections

Thinking With vs. Thinking For — what cognitive offloading really costs you

The capstone — design, build, ethically audit & pitch your own AI tool, defended at viva
